The long-awaited novelty "Crown of Golden Bones" by all of our and everyone's favorite cycle "From Blood and Ashes". I want to say that these are my favorite books. Unfortunately, I read the second part in the electronic version, so there is no review for it, but I will correct it as soon as I have purchased the paper version.

In the third part, we get to know Atlantia more, its inhabitants, customs and traditions, as well as the current king and queen. There are no disturbing events throughout the book, as in the rest of the parts. Everything is going relatively smoothly (at least for me). But at the end the most interesting begins. We finally understand who Poppy is, learn more about the gods and about who belongs to whom. Believe me, you will be completely shocked.

In addition, new heroes appear, but old ones also open up to us from a new side. As for our main couple Poppy and Cas, then everything is as always. Passion, desire, devotion - all at the highest level.

Penelaf in this part has grown before my eyes. Although she sympathized with me from the very beginning, now she is beyond praise. What about Casteel - some of his actions were not entirely correct in relation to the kingdom, but in part I understood him. So I wasn't disappointed in the slightest.

Well, the ending will make you nervous and start waiting for the release of a new book (but here's the catch, the author has frozen the contract for translating the following parts, so it remains to be prayed that all the books will be published). When I closed the last page, no words can describe how many emotions I felt.

Hypothesis of Love By Ali Hazelwood

The many lives of theoretical physicist Elsie Hannaway have finally caught up with her. By day, she’s an adjunct professor, toiling away at grading labs and teaching thermodynamics in the hopes of landing tenure. By other day, Elsie makes up for her non-existent paycheck by offering her services as a fake girlfriend, tapping into her expertly honed people pleasing skills to embody whichever version of herself the client needs.

And that same Jack who now sits on the hiring committee at MIT, right between Elsie and her dream job.

Elsie is prepared for an all-out war of scholarly sabotage but…those long, penetrating looks? Not having to be anything other than her true self when she’s with him? Will falling into an experimentalist’s orbit finally tempt her to put her most guarded theories on love into practice?

Honestly, it’s a pretty sweet gig—until her carefully constructed Elsie-verse comes crashing down. Because Jack Smith, the annoyingly attractive and broody older brother of her favorite client, turns out to be the cold-hearted experimental physicist who ruined her mentor’s career and undermined the reputation of theorists everywhere.

Gods of black hollow By A.Jr.Vrana

Summary: Black Hollow is a mysterious city filled with dark legends. For many centuries, its inhabitants have believed in the legend that a mythical creature, the Dreamer, lures young girls into the forest. One day Elle is found dead on the outskirts of the forest. And this means one thing: the Dreamer has returned. Kai wakes up next to the lifeless body of a recently missing girl and cannot remember what has happened to him in the past few days. The young man is convinced that he did not harm her. However, the terrible secret that he hides can play a cruel joke with him.

Mason comes to Black Hollow to debunk the city's superstitions and plunges headlong into the history of an old legend. The three of them will have to figure out what hides the centuries-old evil that spreads over the foggy forests of Black Hollow.

I got acquainted with this book in the summer, but now is the most suitable time of the year for it. The story is told on behalf of three characters: the girl Mia, who suffers from incomprehensible dreams, the young man Kai, who hides a terrible secret, and the doctor Mason, who fled the city in search of himself and peace.

Even though this story is very creepy in places, it was still impossible not to laugh with Kai and Mia. This couple is something. Sometimes their actions were reckless and defying logic, but thanks to the descriptions of their thoughts, you slowly begin to understand the characters.

So if you are a fan of Maggie Stievoter or love the Supernatural series, then you will definitely like it.

Everyone knows about my love for this book? I mentioned her a lot in stories. Now it's time to write about it.What else can I say? Amazing. Divine. Fabulous. I read the entire book in one sitting on the same day I bought it. I have never been so excited.So far, three books have been published. And I am writing a review for the first part only because I finally ordered the third. I am sure that I will read it in one gulp, but I will not regret it.

So, Penelaf is a virgin, the chosen one. She was lonely and untouchable. But she is also a girl with a soul and a heart.

And from the first pages he is kidnapped by the golden-eyed guardsman Hawk, just like the hearts of all readers. Poppy has a duty to the royal, but Hawk inflames her anger, makes her doubt everything she believes in. Their destinies are now inextricably linked with desire and thirst.

Leafing through page after page, I became more and more immersed in this world and more and more amazed at how beautifully the book was written. The atmosphere, the dialogues of the characters, the plot twists - everything was at the highest level. Also, for me, Penelaf and Hawk have become my favorite characters. Poppy is a smart, brave and, what is important, a strong girl.

Her stubbornness, her steadfastness, her courage cannot but admire. Hawke definitely deserves all the compliments said to him. His devotion to Poppy was amazing. Every scene with him was read in one breath. In short, the best book guy I've ever met. And what chemistry between them! I was touched, then embarrassed by their passion, laughed at their jokes and sarcasm. In general, the spark between Penelaf and Hawk drove me crazy.I still remember those emotions while reading the book. So I highly recommend reading it (although I know that most of you have already read it).

“You are a polyhedron and I am a polyhedron. But that's normal in our system."⠀I have been looking at this book in online stores for a long time, but I bought it only in Moscow (I myself am from Belarus). On the penultimate day, I decided to go to Chitai-gorod and saw her. And I realized that this is fate.

The story is told from the perspective of three characters. Masha is an ordinary girl studying at a prestigious university. She does not communicate with other classmates, preferring Dima's company. He's her best friend and everyone wonders why they're best friends sometimes. But at any moment their relationship can change.

And so it happened when a student from England, Roman, who turns out to be familiar with Dimka, transferred to their group. Only now, every time they meet between them, lightning sparks every now and then.

I want to say that I got pure pleasure from every page. This book is full of comfort and tenderness. It is about true friendship, sincere love, about the values ​​of life. History shows us that we are all imperfect and make mistakes. We can offend a person close to us, although in our hearts we didn’t want it at all. Or we may often regret not taking the first step towards our love because we think that you are very different. But “... both in friendship and in love, it is not necessary to match the edges. You just need not to hurt loved ones on purpose. Even if you think they deserve it. Because they won’t stop being close, and by hurting them, you will hurt yourself…”
