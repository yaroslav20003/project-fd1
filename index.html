Crown of golden bones By Jennifer Armentrout

The long-awaited novelty "Crown of Golden Bones" by all of our and everyone's favorite cycle "From Blood and Ashes". I want to say that these are my favorite books. Unfortunately, I read the second part in the electronic version, so there is no review for it, but I will correct it as soon as I have purchased the paper version.

In the third part, we get to know Atlantia more, its inhabitants, customs and traditions, as well as the current king and queen. There are no disturbing events throughout the book, as in the rest of the parts. Everything is going relatively smoothly (at least for me). But at the end the most interesting begins. We finally understand who Poppy is, learn more about the gods and about who belongs to whom. Believe me, you will be completely shocked.

In addition, new heroes appear, but old ones also open up to us from a new side. As for our main couple Poppy and Cas, then everything is as always. Passion, desire, devotion - all at the highest level.

Penelaf in this part has grown before my eyes. Although she sympathized with me from the very beginning, now she is beyond praise. What about Casteel - some of his actions were not entirely correct in relation to the kingdom, but in part I understood him. So I wasn't disappointed in the slightest.

Well, the ending will make you nervous and start waiting for the release of a new book (but here's the catch, the author has frozen the contract for translating the following parts, so it remains to be prayed that all the books will be published). When I closed the last page, no words can describe how many emotions I felt.

Gods of black hollow By A.Jr.Vrana

Summary: Black Hollow is a mysterious city filled with dark legends. For many centuries, its inhabitants have believed in the legend that a mythical creature, the Dreamer, lures young girls into the forest. One day Elle is found dead on the outskirts of the forest. And this means one thing: the Dreamer has returned. Kai wakes up next to the lifeless body of a recently missing girl and cannot remember what has happened to him in the past few days. The young man is convinced that he did not harm her. However, the terrible secret that he hides can play a cruel joke with him.

Mason comes to Black Hollow to debunk the city's superstitions and plunges headlong into the history of an old legend. The three of them will have to figure out what hides the centuries-old evil that spreads over the foggy forests of Black Hollow.

I got acquainted with this book in the summer, but now is the most suitable time of the year for it. The story is told on behalf of three characters: the girl Mia, who suffers from incomprehensible dreams, the young man Kai, who hides a terrible secret, and the doctor Mason, who fled the city in search of himself and peace.

Even though this story is very creepy in places, it was still impossible not to laugh with Kai and Mia. This couple is something. Sometimes their actions were reckless and defying logic, but thanks to the descriptions of their thoughts, you slowly begin to understand the characters.

So if you are a fan of Maggie Stievoter or love the Supernatural series, then you will definitely like it.

In addition to ordinary books, magical specimens are stored there - grimoires that were created by sorcerers. In addition, on the way you will meet not only them, but also Mentors, demons, demons and other evil spirits.

The main character Elizabeth is the very pupil that grew up among magical grimoires that whisper on the shelves and rattle iron chains. From childhood, she knew and believed that sorcerers were evil. But by the will of fate, she has to resort to the help of one of them.

Nathaniel Thorne, the same sorcerer with the demon servant Silos. I liked him because at least someone in his book did not make a man a pompous male. On the contrary, Nathaniel admired his mind, manners, while still not deprived of strength. But the main character who just stole my heart is Silas. I can talk about him forever. A demon that has a soul, it has such care that not every person can boast of it. His devotion delights, and the second hypostasis touches. I could say a lot about him, but you better get to know him yourself.

Love line - there is nothing to say. Very easy, without any difficulties, but at the same time quite interesting. I especially liked their sarcasm and jokes. Well, besides, no snot and excessive vulgarity.

I was glad I didn't miss this book. I literally read it in one day. So if you want to experience book magic and awe, understandable, perhaps, only to true book lovers, then this story will definitely suit you.
